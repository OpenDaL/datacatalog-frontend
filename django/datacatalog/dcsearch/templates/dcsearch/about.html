Finding the right datasets for a project, research study, or out of curiosity, can be hard. First you have to know what data portals may contain the data, then you need to go to and search each of them, sometimes using limited search functionality. Traditional search engines are generally also of little help, since they only offer keyword based search, and do not let you search for datasets based on properties such as the location it is about, the type of dataset or its subject.

OpenDaL is a search engine for open data and research data, specifically designed to resolve these issues. It combines the datasets from hundreds of data portals in one place, and allows you to search based on aspects like Subject, Format, Location, Temporal Coverage and more. Can't wait to get started? Try it now!

Features

How is OpenDaL different from other search engines?

Metadata Search

In addition to text based search, you can use other dataset properties such as type, subject, language, format and temporal coverage

Location Queries

You can draw a bounding box on the map, to search for relevant data within you area of interest

Combining Portals

You can search hundreds of open data portals, e.g. from governments, research Institutes and universities, all at once
