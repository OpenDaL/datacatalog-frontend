Can't find what you’re looking for? Below are some tips to improve your search query and find the data you need. For more information about each of the search options, please click the icons on the search page.

Metadata Completeness

OpenDaL depends on information from other data portals, which is not always complete. Although we strive to detect missing information, even when it’s not explicitly published, not all resources will have extensive metadata such as a subject, location or dates attached to them. Therefore, if you search for resources with a specific subject, location or creation date, you may get only very little results. In this case, it can help to loosen your search criteria, for example by dropping some requirements, or entering some of them as keywords. For example you can search for the keyword budget rather than the subject budget. Since all resources in OpenDaL have a title, and most have descriptions, this will likely generate more results.

Layered Queries

For both the 'Type' and 'Subject' fields, OpenDaL uses a layered system. If you for example select Macro Economy as a subject it will also query for underlying subjects (such as Employment, Economic Development and Income). In the subject list, you can see what subjects are included in others, by looking at their indentation. The 'Type' field is layered similarly. For example querying for a Document also queries for Document:Paper, Document:report, and more. In case you don't find the data you're looking for when searching for a very specific (low layer) subject, it may be better to search using a higher layer. For example, because not all data portals use very specific definitions, data about the subject Trade data may be classified as the more general subject Economy.

Advanced Text Search

You can use advanced queries in the search box. In OpenDaL you can use operators '+' (for AND), '|' (for OR) and '-' (for NOT), asterisks (*) to look for prefixes, semicolons to look for specific phrases, and brackets to group parts of a query. For example the query bathymetry (seafloor | river) australia* "geomorphic features" -"marine park" looks for entries with the word bathymetry AND (seafloor OR river), that include any word starting with australia (e.g. australia's or australian) and contains the exact phrase 'geomorphic features' but does not include the phrase "marine park".
